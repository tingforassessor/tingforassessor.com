What is the Tulsa County Assessors office?

The County Assessor's only function is the fair and equitable assessment of property and therefore placing a Fair Market Value on all property within Tulsa County as of the tax date, which is January 1 of every year. The type of properties that the Assessor's office is tasked to appraise consists of Commercial properties, Residential properties, Agricultural properties, and Business Personal property.
Each dollar derived from property taxes in Tulsa County is apportioned by about 75% for schools and 10% to fund county operations, with the remaining percentage apportioned to other public entities and publicly supported projects as authorized by the voters.

For the past 10 years and counting I have made my career as a Real Property Appraiser for the Tulsa County Assessor’s office. This year I have made the most important decision of my career and perhaps my life, which is to run for this public office.

This Is My Vision

My plan is one that is rooted in the best technology for the staff to perform their jobs to the highest quality resulting in accuracy, equality, and efficiency. We all know that we are living in a time when technology is disrupting, as well as, improving entire industries and mass property appraisal is certainly one that is ripe for disruptive improvement. I have been a tech geek at heart my whole life and I will put together an IT team that will build the finest software that will essentially automate the tasks and responsibilities for all departments of the TCA office in its entirety.

I have been in this line of work for over a decade and I have always kept up with other counties in the country that are on the cutting edge of technology for mass appraisal. I am absolutely confident that we can build software that will exceed the quality of even the most progressive counties such as King County Washington, San Francisco County California, and all of the other largest counties. Sound like a bold claim? Yes, it is but I vow that we can accomplish this within only one election term of 4 years.

My goal is to make sure that the biggest, most valuable commercial property buildings are valued just as accurately as the smallest, most common residential house. There are millions, if not tens of millions, of unreceived property tax dollars mainly from the commercial properties that are owned by the largest out-of-town corporations and REITs (Real Estate Investment Trusts). The long standing problem with the office culture of past administrations' management is that Appraisers were not provided good technology to do their jobs with nor have they been encouraged to accurately value these multi-million dollar properties and then defend their value when these conglomerate corportions would send in their attorneys and tax representatives to beat down the value, therefore reducing the amount of tax dollars paid. Also, the appraisers are not discouraged from making a reduction for these corporations so that the appraiser and supervisors wouldn't have to worry about defending our values when they would get appealed to the next highest level of the court system.

It is your right as a property owner to protest the current valuation that the Assessors office places on your property and this process is perhaps the most difficult and daunting part of the appraisers’ job. As the Assessor, I vow to staff the office with the highest qualified, toughest, and fairest appraiser’s that will value these commercial properties at a true FAIR MARKET VALUE and steadfastly stand by their valuation even through the protest process.
